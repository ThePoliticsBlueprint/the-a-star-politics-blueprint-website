What is parliamentary sovereignty?

The principle that Parliament has supreme legal authority in the UK and can create or abolish any law. No other body can overrule its legislation.

What is First Past the Post (FPTP)?

Electoral system where the candidate with the most votes wins, regardless of whether they have a majority. Used for UK general elections.

What is collective ministerial responsibility?

Cabinet ministers must publicly support all government decisions or resign. Ensures government unity and maintains confidence.
